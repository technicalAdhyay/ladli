Cause/Concept Introduction:

Throughout the world, COVID-19 pandemic is a crisis like no other the world has faced in recent decades in terms of its potential and social impact.

On 24 March 2020, the Indian government announced a three-week nationwide lockdown to contain the spread of corona virus in the country. The scope of this order is hard to comprehend: India is a country of more than a billion people; it suffers from extreme poverty in several areas and many of its cities such as Delhi, Mumbai and Kolkata are extraordinarily dense. This inevitably left hundreds of thousands of migrant workers stranded; many of them were compelled to return home by covering hundreds of kilometers on foot as the transportation services were suspended.

During the pandemic lockdown, key problems arose in terms of hunger, poverty and hygiene. Although, India actually may have enough food to feed its entire population, just from the public stock, it’s not clear how the food will get to those places in such a short period of time. Hence, despite having good intentions that focus on welfare of the common people, the execution of it can be challenging in a country like India, because of the numerous logistical obstacles and hurdles.

When it comes to personal health & hygiene - women are facing the brunt of the lockdown when compared to men. During the initial phase of the lockdown, sanitary napkins were not included in the list of essential items. This left many women and girls with no choice but to resort to the age-old, unhygienic practice of using old clothes to manage their periods due to limited access to sanitary pads and broken supply-chain for menstrual hygiene products, particularly the underprivileged class.

Ladli Foundation Trust being a helping hand to fight against pandemic through Project – RAHAT SEVA

To conquer the hard times of pandemic lockdown, a nationwide campaign was conducted in association with India News – SATHI HATH BADHANA. We worked towards the distribution of essential rations and dry food, masks, hand sanitizers, stationary to children, and sanitary kits, to provide relief from the fear of pandemic with the support of Delhi Police, ONGC, Jindal Stainless Limited Group, Pertronet LNG and Ritu Kumar in various slums of Delhi.
Ladli was able to achieve some enormous benchmarks to provide relief against nationwide pandemic:

  • Over 1 lakh migrant labourers were directly benefitted during the difficult and trying times with the support of Delhi Police
  • Distributed 1-month dry ration kits to 20000 families.
  • Effective ground presence of police staff helped in maintaining social distancing.
  • Distributed 1.5 lakh cotton mask
  • 1.5 lakh sanitary pads to underprivileged females and other menstruators.
  • Distributed Lactogen Baby Milk Powder for 6000 new born babies in underprivileged areas like Ambedkar Nagar, Fatehpur beri, Madhu vihar, Gwala Dairy, Durgavihar (Dwarka), Rangpuri Pahadi (Vasant Kunj) etc.
  • Supplied Saheli Sanitary kits to underprivileged girls and women in the slum areas of Kalkaji where the numbers of beneficiaries for nearly 300+ people.

While our volunteers were working on field to provide help to all those in need during the times of the COVID-19 pandemic, we discovered that out of all the classes and cross-sections of the society, it was the mothers of new born babies in slums who have suffered perhaps the most during the period of pandemic lockdown.

We found that they did not even have enough money to be able to afford a cup of tea. They were destitute, they had nothing. There were people akin to our organization who distributed ration and essential items to them. Sometimes they got food, sometimes they didn’t. But the matter of most concern was about the newly born babies – they were unable to eat anything and milk packet supplies were disrupted. Women living in slums are often weak and anemic, they are often unable to lactate in that condition and therefore can’t breastfeed their babies. What about their health and their child’s life?
The Road ahead:

While government schemes ensured that the poor would get additional rations due to the lockdown, the distribution system failed to be effective as the ration cards are area-specific and fair price shops were largely inaccessible. Various Non-Governmental Organization such as Ladli foundation have taken a step forward and spearheaded the initiative to provide these migrant workers and other vulnerable sectors with basic amenities, transportation provisions and other benefits. Now, with the things turning back to normal, at a slow pace, our attempt is to re-establish these workers with better life skills, to adjust to the ‘new normal’.
