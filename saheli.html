Cause Introduction

A large number of vulnerable populations is residing in urban & rural slums of India are sustaining below the poverty line index and are uneducated, unaware and deprived of basic amenities. These families are often form the lowest rung of the society and particularly in this stratum of social and financial order women are the poorest of the poor with minimal right of choice and no right of expression.

Women from this stratum are still struggling to acknowledge their fundamental rights and have no access to adequate resources for voicing their opinions even for their healthcare and basic rights. We believe that sexual and reproductive health often falls between the cracks wherein women are not in a strong position to make or influence personal decisions.

Saheli is an Action-Research initiative that is especially designed for a holistic development of underprivileged women and girls in which we have successfully organized numbers of mega health camps , counselling sessions and intensive community outreach activities for sensitizing the female population about the most concerning issues related to MHM, WASH, Sexual Abuse and prevention of life threatening communicable diseases, HIV, COVID-19 & STD’s through medical screening and involving mothers and daughters together to make them understand the importance of their bonding to discuss the most sensitives issues to eliminate them at initial stage.

The project is designed in a way to start a conversation among people living in slums where inhuman traditional practices, myths, taboos and poor health conditions which are the main hurdles in providing them basic amenities and equal rights and opportunities for women.

It aims to alter all about break the taboos and adopt the safe and sustainable healthcare and reproductive rights for women. So that they drop the shame attached to these issues and talk about them without any hesitation and to create attention to the growing gender inequality and discusses how it is cultural and not biological

Beneficiaries

We reach out to the female members of the community who presently belong to menstruating age groups, as well as those who are in their pre-menarche stage. The project will also reach out to young boys and girls in schools to educate them on wash rights which is very much important for good menstruation practices and to break the taboo which is attached to it. We’ve chosen this target group for there are prevalent examples of the feminization of poverty in backward regions of India making a large chunk of underprivileged women financially and socially crippled. It is very much imperative that the identified target group will help in drawing out strategies to initiate discourse, and educate and trigger behaviour change.

A majority of these girls are sexually victimized and are forced into involuntary prostitution for the sake of their survival. Oblivious to their sexual and reproductive rights, as well as the importance of feminine hygiene, women in the underprivileged sector are susceptible to numerous health issues like anaemia, unwanted pregnancy, UTI, cancer, and HIV. These women end up becoming carriers of life-threatening Sexually Transmitted Diseases like HIV AIDS, Hepatitis, Chancroid, etc. which further spread in the community due to multiple sexual partners. Moreover, we aim to include men in these conversations to encourage their acknowledgment and participation in these issues which will help these women to talk about their issues and address them.

Till now we have directly benefitted 2.75 Lakhs females by providing primary healthcare and sensitized over 6 lakh females across India.
